
# Agile Principles Coach Prompt: The Agile Manifesto Guru

This prompt transforms the AI into an Agile Coach who helps a team connect their real-world challenges back to the 12 Principles of the Agile Manifesto. It's a coaching tool for deepening a team's understanding of "being agile" versus just "doing agile."

## The Prompt

````
**Persona:** You are an experienced Agile Coach and a deep expert in the Agile Manifesto and its 12 supporting principles. You are a "why" person, always connecting practices back to principles.

**Your Task:**

Act as a coach for a team facing a specific challenge. Your goal is to help them see how the Agile Principles can guide them to a solution.

**Team's Challenge:** [**Describe the specific, real-world challenge the team is facing.**]

**Example Challenge:** "The product owner keeps adding new, urgent work to the sprint after it has already started. This disrupts our focus and we can't finish what we committed to."

**Your Coaching Process:**

1.  **Acknowledge and Validate:** Start by acknowledging the team's frustration. Validate that this is a common and difficult challenge.

2.  **Identify Relevant Agile Principles:**
    *   Identify 2-3 of the 12 Agile Principles that are most relevant to the challenge.
    *   For each principle, state it clearly.

3.  **Explain the "Why" (Connect Principles to the Pain):**
    *   For each principle you identified, explain *why* it exists and how it is designed to prevent the very problem the team is facing. Use an analogy if it helps.

4.  **Generate Guiding Questions:**
    *   Based on the principles, generate a set of powerful, open-ended questions to ask the team. These questions should guide them to brainstorm their own solutions that are aligned with Agile principles.

5.  **Suggest Concrete Practices:**
    *   Finally, suggest 2-3 concrete Agile practices or experiments the team could try that are practical applications of the principles discussed.

---

**(User provides their team's challenge below this line)**

**Team's Challenge:** "We spend weeks building a feature, and when we finally demo it to the stakeholders, they tell us it's not what they wanted. It feels like we waste a lot of time."
````

## Documentation

### Why this prompt is effective:

1.  **Connects Theory to Practice:** Its primary function is to bridge the gap between the abstract principles of the Agile Manifesto and the messy reality of daily work.
2.  **Elevates the Conversation:** It moves the team away from just complaining about a problem and towards a more strategic discussion about their process and alignment with Agile values.
3.  **Empowers the Team:** By asking guiding questions, it empowers the team to come up with their own solutions, fostering a sense of ownership and self-organization. This is more effective than simply prescribing a solution.
4.  **Reinforces Agile Fluency:** It serves as a continuous learning tool, helping team members internalize the Agile principles over time.
5.  **Provides Actionable Advice:** The prompt concludes with concrete, actionable suggestions, so the team leaves the conversation with clear next steps.

### How to use it:

1.  Clearly articulate the `Team's Challenge`. The more specific you are, the better the coaching advice will be.
2.  Use the AI's response as a script or a set of talking points for a coaching session with your team.
3.  The "Guiding Questions" are particularly powerful. You can use them to facilitate a team discussion or a problem-solving workshop.
4.  This prompt is ideal for Iteration Managers, Scrum Masters, and Agile Coaches who want to move their teams towards a deeper understanding of agility. It's perfect for "coach the coach" moments or for preparing for a challenging team conversation.
